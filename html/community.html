Community Impact

One Boston Day is an opportunity for businesses and organizations to display their humanity and bring neighbors together. Here are a few ways the Boston business and non-profit community got involved in 2016:

St. Francis House partnered with John Hancock to collect sneakers for the many guests they serve.

Learn more

Boston Marathon survivors and founders of the Gillian Reny Stepping Strong Fund hosted a blood drive just past the Marathon finish line on Boylston Street.

Learn more

Legacy Place in Dedham hosted a One Boston Day walk to benefit the Greater Boston Food Bank.

Learn more

The Museum of Science offered free entry for visitors to reflect in the peace of the Butterfly Garden.

Learn more

The City of Boston collected donations of pre-mixed baby formula, baby wipes, and hand sanitizer for Flint, Michigan.

Learn more

El Pelon donated $1 from every burrito sold on One Boston Day to the Massachusetts Immigrant and Refugee Advocacy Coalition.

Learn more

The Pine Street Inn had a dry cereal drive.
